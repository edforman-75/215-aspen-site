Design Brief

Direction, constraints, and decisions

Direction

Mid-Century Modern Meets Warm Minimalism

Clean lines, natural materials, nothing fussy. The stone fireplace, exposed wood beams, hardwood floors, mountain views, and collected textiles from around the world are the stars. Everything else supports them.

Primary furniture source: Room & Board — American-made, walnut and performance fabrics throughout.

Paint: Benjamin Moore White Dove — sample on wall near fireplace and near window before committing.

Inventory

What Stays

  • 1918 Steinway Model O baby grand — east corner, morning light, Baldy views
  • Wall hangings & textiles — kente cloth, woven baskets, red patchwork, Southwest weavings, Guy Weir prints
  • Freestanding stone fireplace — hearth ledge with stored cushions seats 2–3
  • TV on pivot mount — between fireplace and front door
  • Kitchen & island — maple cabinets, curved concrete island, unchanged
  • Track lighting — existing tracks stay; heads can be swapped
  • Triangular heirloom side table — accent piece
  • Hardwood floors — unchanged
  • Mahogany buffet — on the 9' buffet wall
  • Rugs — possibly keep, open to replacing

What Goes

All current sofas, chairs, both dining tables, lamps, coffee table, sideboard, media console, counter stools, Southwest pottery lamp, wrought iron chandelier, wrought iron accessories, and most decorative items.

Zones

Five Activity Zones

Zone 1 Living / Seating Area

Center of room, oriented toward fireplace and TV wall. Sofa faces fireplace, two accent chairs flank the arrangement, walnut coffee table anchors grouping, area rug defines zone.

Meeting mode (16 people): Chairs reposition to face TV, dining chairs from both tables pulled in, counter stools repositioned, hearth cushions out. All movable by one person.

Zone 2 Primary Dining + Workstation 1

Left of kitchen island facing cabinets, under chandelier replacement. Adams Extension Table seats 4 daily, expands to 8–10. As workstation: laptop + monitor at end of table facing Baldy/Warm Springs, backlight from sliders behind. Tech tucked away for dinner.

Zone 3 Piano

East corner, unchanged. Windows on two sides, views of Baldy, soft morning light. Floor lamp for sheet music replaces Southwest pottery lamp. Piano bench stays. Corner kept clear and uncluttered — the piano is both instrument and sculpture.

Zone 4 Reading / Workstation 2 / Overflow Dining

Near top of stairs, passage wall area. Adams Drop-Leaf Table — leaves down = slim desk, leaves up = overflow dining for 4–6. Triple duty: workspace, reading area, overflow dining for large gatherings. Combined with Zone 2's table, seats 14–16 for major dinner parties.

Zone 5 Kitchen Counter Seating

Along curved concrete island, facing kitchen. Three new counter stools replace wrought iron ones. Casual breakfast, party perching, meeting overflow.

Requirements

Special Constraints

  • All seating individually movable — no heavy sectionals
  • Dining chairs comfortable enough for meetings
  • Two workstation locations, both facing Baldy/Warm Springs
  • Convertible second dining surface for 10+ dinner parties
  • IKEA folding chairs stored for overflow seating
  • 36"+ clear passages on both sides of fireplace
  • Performance fabrics throughout — rental-grade durability
