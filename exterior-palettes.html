Fixed Element · Beige Window & Slider Cladding

Warm beige aluminum cladding — all kitchen windows and sliders. Cannot be painted. Approximately matches BM Sandy Brown 2164-40 or SW Latte. Every palette must harmonize with this warm beige as the de facto trim color.

Integral Color in Smooth Coat

If you smooth-coat the stucco, the body color can be mixed directly into the finish coat — no painting needed. This is more durable, won't peel, and reads as a true material rather than a painted surface. The brick would still need to be painted separately to match. Ask the stucco contractor about integral pigment in a smooth trowel finish (sometimes called "Santa Barbara" or "California" finish).

1 Warm Oyster · Taupe-Mauve

Quiet sophistication — "Is that grey or mauve?" The beige cladding blends in.

The safest path. The mauve is barely there — more of a warm taupe with a rosy whisper. Against snow it reads warm and grounded; in summer among aspens it recedes elegantly. The beige cladding nearly disappears into the body color. This says "expensive taste" without saying anything loud.

Why it works: Smoked Oyster has brown, violet, and grey undertones — a sophisticated taupe that reads mauve in afternoon light. LRV of 22 is medium-dark, adding gravitas. The beige cladding sits naturally in the warm family. Espresso door creates the only real contrast — clean mid-century move.
2 Desert Rose · Warm Mauve

Unmistakably mauve — warm, earthy, confident. The beige cladding becomes a deliberate accent.

This is the "yes, that's mauve" option. Hint of Mauve is a true dusty rose-taupe — you'll see the pink, especially in mountain light. It's warm enough to feel right with the beige cladding (they share the same warm base), and the LRV of 42 means it won't go dark or gloomy in winter. This is a house that knows what it wants.

3 Plum Dusk · Deep Mauve

Bold and dramatic — the house becomes a presence against the mountain. The beige cladding glows.

The statement palette. Dusty Mauve is a true rose-brown with real depth — it will read differently at every hour, from rosy-warm in morning sun to almost plum at dusk. Against snow it's stunning; the beige cladding catches light by contrast. This is the palette if you want the house to announce itself. Not for the timid — but it's gorgeous.

Next Steps

1. Order peel-and-stick samples from Samplize.com — all three body colors + their soffit/door companions. About $6 each. Stick them on the brick AND the stucco in full sun, shade, and dusk.

2. Hold samples next to the beige cladding — this is the critical test. The cladding should feel like it belongs, not like an afterthought.

3. Look at them in snow light — winter is your dominant season visually. Colors shift cool against white ground.

4. Ask the stucco contractor about integral pigment smooth coat — if feasible, it's the most durable and mid-century-authentic approach.
